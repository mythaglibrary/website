## !$@

### +1/+2/+3/…

When referring to an awakener or wheel, it means extra copies after E3, using the number that is shown in the UI. For example if you have 6 copies of Ramona, you have E3+2, or a +2 Ramona.

## 0-9

## A

### A1/A2/A3/A4/A5

Stands for Alert 1, 2, 3, etc. in D-Effect Zone.

### AA

Absolute Axiom. Minor buff that unlocks when you have a character at +12.

### AOE

"Area of effect." Something that affects all enemies at once.

### April

The covenant [April Tribute](/handbook/team#april-tribute).

### Arith

Arithmetica.

## B

### Baby

Embryo (the Caro special card).

### Battery

An awakener that can generate aliemus for other awakeners.

### Battle Pass/BP

Curriculum (the seasonal reward track you can unlock with real money).

### Black pool

Using a rare item called Origin of Time to reset a standard character, refunding all their enlightens and level up materials and removing them from your account, thus "returning them to the Black Pool."

### Blade

Blade of the Titan (Goliath's SSR wheel).

### Blue

Aequor.

### Bluwil/Bluewil

An Aequor team using Tawil as the main damage dealer.

### Buff

A bonus or positive status effect.

### Burial

The covenant [Burial Ground's Sighs](/handbook/team#burial-grounds-sighs).

### Burst damage

Damage which is dealt all in a single turn.

## C

### Carry/Solo carry

An awakener who "carries" their team over the finish line. Without them, the team doesn't work.

### Chains

Chains Unbound (Helot's SSR wheel). Can also refer to the Chains of Resentment effect of certain enemies, which seals awakeners that hit them.

### Clem

Clementine.

### Cocoon

An effect on some enemies that grants them increasing fortress (damage reduction) the more damage dealt to them in a turn. Can also refer to the [Cocoon of the Maiden](/handbook/team#cocoon-of-the-maiden) covenant set.

### Corpo

Corposant.

### Costco

In Faded Legacy stages, a strategy based on buying lots of relics using the wheels Frenzy and Emerge.

### Cov

Covenant.

## D

### Daff

Daffodil.

### Debuff

A negative status effect.

### Divine Realm

Aequor (Divine Aequor/DAequor): When some characters like GMurphy are in your team, they change Aequor into "Divine Realm: Aequor," which works differently. Tentacle DMG is now based on your max HP; your DMG amplification is increased by 50%; exalts no longer give Tentacle Gathering; and the stances are stronger but have a 3-turn cooldown.

### Divine Ultra

See "Singularity: Ultra."

### Doorstain

Doresain, who is much maligned because he's bad in endgame content.

### Dore

Doresain.

### DPS

"Damage per second." Term from gaming slang. Can refer to the amount of damage an awakener does, or to an awakener whose role is main damage dealer.

### Dual wield

Using two SSR wheels at once. You can do this if one of them is at +12.

### DR

Death Resistance.

### Dream/Dreams

The covenant [Dream of Medicine](/handbook/team#dream-of-medicine). Used to have various different English names.

### D-Tide/Dtide

D-Effect Zone. Abbreviation of the old English translation.

## E

### E0/E1/E2/E3

Enlighten 0/1/2/3.

### Ecog

Emergency Gnosis. Abbreviation of the old English translation.

### Embers

Ancient Embers, an effect some high-HP enemies have. They take a certain amount of extra damage each turn, so their effective HP is lower, but they're harder to kill in one turn with burst damage.

## F

### Feast

The covenant [Feast from Afar](/handbook/team#feast-from-afar).

### Fin

Fin of Sorrow (Caecus's SSR wheel).

### Furnace

Crimson Furnace (the Caro mechanic).

## G

### GDoll

Doll: Inferno. Stands for "Genesis Doll."

### GHelot/Gelot

Helot: Catena. Stands for "Genesis Helot."

### GMurphy/Gurphy

Murphy: Fauxborn. Stands for "Genesis Murphy."

### Goli

Goliath.

### GRamona

Ramona: Timeworn. Stands for "Genesis Ramona."

## H

### Ham

Hameln.

### HSR

Hectic Skybound Rail. (Not to be confused with a certain other gacha game.)

## I

### Image

Dimensional Image Relic. Refers to a series of rare relics in Astral Reign stages that each power up a specific awakener.

### Incalc

Incalculable Factor (Winkle's SSR wheel).

## J

### JP Ultra

See "Singularity: Ultra." Refers to the fact that this version of Ultra is ported from the Japanese release of Morimens.

## K

### Kath

Kathigu-Ra.

### Keyflare bot

A support awakener whose main job is to hold the covenant [Life Drain](/handbook/team#life-drain) and provide keyflare for the team.

## L

### Lighthouse

Amidst the Downpour (Faros's SSR wheel).

## M

### Mana

Arithmetica. Term from Magic: the Gathering/Hearthstone.

### Mob/Mobs

Normal enemies (as opposed to elites and bosses). Term from gaming slang.

### Mono

Refers to a team comprised of characters from only one realm.

### Monowil

A Pure Chaos team using Tawil as the main damage dealer.

### Monthly dupes

Refers to Prototype Horizon and Timeloop Copy, two items in the Store that grant duplicates of any owned character or wheel, including limited ones.

## N

### Naut

Nautila.

### Nerf

To make something weaker after a game update.

## O

### OE

Over-Exaltation. When an awakener is at +4 (7 dupes) or more, they can use a powered-up exalt which costs 200 aliemus.

### OP

Overpowered; much more powerful than other options.

## P

### P-Dive/Pdive

Phantasmal Dive.

### Pen

Remembrance Quill (material used for locking substats in covenant transcription).

### Pizza

Covenant.

### Plug

Refined Gnosis Primer (material used for leveling up a character beyond 70).

### Portrait

See "Image."

### Premium support

A powerful, game-changing supportive character such as Thais. Usually used in the context of D-Effect Zone where you can't repeat characters, so you have to decide where you're putting your premium supports.

### Preorder

A time-limited paid bundle that sometimes appears when new characters are released. Rewards unlock over time, but you get bonus cosmetics for buying the bundle early.

### Proc

Trigger an effect. Comes from gaming slang.

### Purple

Ultra.

### Pyramid

Can refer to either Gnosis Primer (material used for leveling up a character beyond 60) or Origin of Time (a rare item given out during events and used to reset characters).

## Q

## R

### Racism

Tongue-in-cheek term for when a character is stronger against specific enemies. For example, Corposant is infamously "racist" against Aequor enemies in Astral Reign because her soulforge talent makes her deal more damage to them.

### Rat wheel

Path Forsaken (Jenkin's SSR wheel).

### Red

Caro.

### Retal

Counter. Abbreviation of the old English translation.

## S

### Salv

Salvador.

### Scale/Scaling

The ability to grow in power over the course of a battle or stage. Alternatively, the ability to grow in power based on a specific resource, such as STR.

### SF

Soulforge Aptitude (talent which boosts stats in Astral Reign stages).

### Singularity: Ultra (SUltra)

When some characters like Arachne are in your team, they change Ultra into "Singularity: Ultra," which works differently. Your DMG amplification is increased by 50%; your command cards are buffed and get further buffs when put into Ultra Space; Annihilation has a 3-turn cooldown and puts the leftmost card from Ultra Space into your hand instead of giving you an Insight; and the Ultra Round extends your current turn instead of starting a new turn.

### Skey

Keyflare. Abbreviation of the old English translation.

### Skey bot

See "Keyflare bot."

### Spam/Spamming

Using the same ability or card over and over again.

### Stamp

Thousand Faces Seal (material used for upgrading and transcribing covenants).

### Stat stick/_ stick

Refers to awakeners or wheels that are used because of their high basic attributes rather than their actual abilities. For example: "Even if you don't care about the effect of Path Forsaken, you can still use it as a Crit Rate stick."

### Sub DPS

A secondary damage dealer in addition to the main DPS.

### Sustain

The ability of a team to stay alive over many turns through healing and shielding.

### Sustained damage

Damage which is dealt consistently over multiple turns.

## T

### Tank/Tanking

A "tanky" awakener can take incoming damage or negative status effects without consequence.

### Tawheel

Wheel Unseen (Tawil's SSR wheel).

## U

### Unit

Awakener. Comes from gaming slang.

### Utility

Useful abilities that awakeners have apart from dealing damage.

## V

### Vort/Vortex

Vortice.

## W

### W1/W2/W3/W4/W5

Stands for Wave 1, 2, 3, etc. in D-Effect Zone.

### Wheel jail

Pulling on the standard Wheels of Rotation banner to get a wheel to +12.
