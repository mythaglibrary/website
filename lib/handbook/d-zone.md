# D-Zone For Dummies

![](/images/handbook/d-zone/d-zone.png){width="117.95" loading=lazy}
![](/images/handbook/d-zone/regional-d-effect-zone.png){width="384" loading=lazy}


Regional D-Effect Zone (a.k.a. "D-tide") is the main repeatable endgame content in Morimens.

In this game mode, you have to clear up to 5 separate waves (stages) without repeating any characters or wheels. The more waves you clear at higher difficulties, the more rewards you receive.

## D-Tide Is Not Scary

**Try to do D-tide as soon as you unlock it.**

New players tend to ignore D-tide because it's "endgame content." In fact, you can and should start as soon as you have the opportunity. This is because even a new player can clear the lowest difficulty level and get tons of Silver and level-up materials.

By starting D-tide early, you'll get resources to level up faster, you'll get more experienced at the game mode, and you'll be able to get more and more rewards from D-tide in the future.

## Choosing Your Difficulty

OK, so you want to give D-tide a shot. What difficulty should you aim for? Here's a rough estimate of what you need to clear each alert.

## Rules of D-Tide

### Scoring

Each D-tide rotation begins on Monday and lasts 2 weeks. You have those 2 weeks to clear 5 waves and get as many points as possible.

You don't have to do all the waves at the same time, and you can reset and retry as often as you want. Only your highest score will be counted at the end of the season.

Clearing all waves at Alert 1 will give 300 points and about half of the total rewards available. Higher difficulties will give more points and more rewards, up to five Alert 5 clears, which gives 400 points and all rewards.

You can earn more points by doing **extra clears**. Each wave can be cleared one extra time with a new team to get +5 points. In other words, you can do two clears of a wave at Alert 1, and it will give the same number of points as one clear at Alert 2.

(You can even extra clear Alert 5 five times to get 425 points. This is very difficult, gives no rewards, and is mainly done by veteran players for bragging rights.)

As you can see, **clearing more waves gives more points than clearing one wave at a higher difficulty**. This is why you might want to invest horizontally rather than sinking all your resources into one team. You can take as long as you want to clear the main story, but if you miss rewards from a D-tide rotation, they're gone forever.

### Team Restrictions

**You can't repeat characters or wheels in D-tide**. Once you use a character or wheel to clear a wave, it's "locked in" for that clear, and you have to reset that clear if you want to use it somewhere else.

**You also can't repeat posses**, but this is less of an issue, since different teams tend to want different posses anyway.

[Covenants](/handbook/team#building-covenants) can be freely reused, so you can build just a few sets and use them for all your D-tide clears.

For one of your clears, [you can and should borrow a support character](#claim-your-free-alert-5-clear).

### Ruleset

D-tide uses [Astral Reign](/handbook/storylines#astral-reign-arc-2) rules, which may be unfamiliar if you're a new player. Make sure you understand how these are different from [Faded Legacy](/handbook/storylines#faded-legacy-arc-1) rules.

In particular, make sure you have at least [soulforge level 1](/handbook/resources#soulforge-aptitude) on all the characters you're using.

### Relics

![](/images/handbook/d-zone/relics.png){width="384" loading=lazy}

Each wave in D-tide gives you two free relics when you start the investigation.

The first relic is an [Astral Reign](/handbook/storylines#astral-reign-arc-2) relic which is unique to that wave. This relic will be more useful for certain teams and strategies, giving those teams and strategies a stronger matchup into that wave.

Sometimes the relic also enables new strategies. For example, if it generates poison or counter and can act as a source of damage, you can run a low-DPS [stall team](/handbook/team#stall) that otherwise wouldn't be able to clear.

![](/images/handbook/d-zone/realm-ring.png){width="384" loading=lazy}

The second relic will always be either Chaos Ring, Aequor Ring, Caro Ring, or Ultra Ring. This is a special relic which rotates every D-tide season and buffs characters from a specific realm.

If you use any characters from the specified realm, you get their Dimensional Image Relic for free. This is a special relic which gives that character 15 aliemus per turn, and also has a bonus effect depending on the character.

In most cases, a character's image relic will move them up "half a tier" on the tier list. Some characters, like [GMurphy](/handbook/awakeners#gmurphy), [Xu](/handbook/awakeners#xu), and [Arachne](/handbook/awakeners#arachne), have especially powerful image relics that make them meta-defining during that D-tide rotation.

**Even standard units become powerful when their Ring is active**. This is why [all the standard units are endgame viable](/handbook/resources#your-first-awakening) and why [you should pull all the realms rather than sticking to one realm](/handbook/resources#pure-cores-standard-pulls).

![](/images/handbook/d-zone/stage-effect-description.png){width="384" loading=lazy}

You can see what each Dimensional Image does by clicking on the "Astral Reign" banner, then "Stage Relics," and finally scrolling down to the "Dimensional Image" section.

## Know Your Matchups

Before you dive into D-tide, plan your strategy by checking the relic, enemies, and boss of each wave. Different conditions are going to favor different team comps.

- If the boss has only one HP bar, it favors Max HP % damage characters like OE [Lotan](/handbook/awakeners#lotan).
- If the boss uses multihit attacks, it favors STR down and counter DPS like [Caecus](/handbook/awakeners#caecus).
- If the boss summons more enemies, it favors DPS with good AOE like [Kathigu-Ra](/handbook/awakeners#kathigu-ra).
- If the boss has Ancient Embers, it favors DPS that deal damage every turn like [Vortice](/handbook/awakeners#vortice).
- If the boss seals you or has other annoying restrictions, it favors teams that can ignore or bypass those restrictions.

If you don't know what the boss does, you can watch replays from the leaderboard, or check the [Morimens Boss Compendium](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1QYciqHJY3BilDcdvBiIT_dOqXOdqGX-4FBDj5rL6qyA/){target="_blank"}, or ask in [one of the community Discords](https://discord.gg/RAegY8wcGx){target="_blank"}, or scout the stage at a lower difficulty first.

Each wave also has a roughly consistent length:

- Wave 1 is a **short exploration stage** where you can skip to the boss early.
- Waves 2 and 5 are **long exploration stages** with many fights and multiple shops.
- Wave 4 is always **2 elites and a boss** with no mob fights and 1 shop.
- Wave 3 is always **a single boss fight** with no mob fights and no shops.

Long exploration stages favor characters that grow stronger after each fight, such as [Ryker](/handbook/awakeners#ryker) and [Pollux](/handbook/awakeners#pollux). Boss-only stages favor characters that have trouble with mob fights, such as [GDoll](/handbook/awakeners#gdoll) and most poison characters.

## Starve Your Teams

<figure markdown="span">
  ![](/images/emojis/agrippa-crying.png){width="128" loading=lazy} <figcaption>"Where is my food? This is an outrage!"</figcaption>
</figure>

In D-tide, you don't get prizes for clearing stages fast or overkilling bosses. The only things that matter are:

- whether you clear the stage or not, and
- how many waves you can clear.

If you use all your good characters and wheels on one team, you may have an easier time doing that wave. But you'll have nothing left for your other teams, so they'll struggle more and you may actually end up with less points.

The smart strategy is to split your good characters and wheels across as many teams as possible. In other words, **make the worst possible team that can still clear each wave**.

- Which gives more points: clearing one wave at Alert 5 with a team of [GHelot](/handbook/awakeners#ghelot), [Thais](/handbook/awakeners#thais), [Salvador](/handbook/awakeners#salvador), and [Arachne](/handbook/awakeners#arachne), or clearing four waves at Alert 1 with each of those characters individually?
- Do you *need* [Thais](/handbook/awakeners#thais) to clear that wave, or can you substitute a less powerful support and still clear?
- Do you *need* the extra damage from Blade of the Titan, or can you substitute a less powerful wheel?
- In fact, do you *need* to put wheels on your supports at all?

Be ruthless. Remember you can clear Alert 3 and below with scraps. The better you get at starving your teams, the more waves you'll be able to clear with your limited resources, and the more rewards you'll be able to get every D-tide.

For more information on teambuilding, see the [How to Build a Team](/handbook/team) section of this guide.

## Claim Your Free Alert 5 Clear

For one of your clears each D-tide rotation, you're allowed to borrow a support character from another player. This works the same way as borrowing a support character in events and Phantasmal Dive. [If you favorite them, they don't have to follow you back.](https://www.reddit.com/r/Morimens/comments/1r2lmmw/how_to_kidnap_a_whale_supporter/){target="_blank"}

What this practically means is that in addition to whatever characters are on your account, you are also allowed to use one free, fully maxed, +12 level 90 limited carry with whatever wheels and covenants you want.

If you're new, I recommend borrowing a [Mouchette](/handbook/awakeners#mouchette) to clear one wave at Alert 5 difficulty. It's literally free points.

(If it's a bad matchup for [Mouchette](/handbook/awakeners#mouchette), you can also borrow a [Pollux](/handbook/awakeners#pollux), [GHelot](/handbook/awakeners#ghelot), etc.)

## Cheri's Flowchart of Diminishing Respect

Personally, I follow this strategy for my D-tide clears:

**Step 1**. I figure out which wave is the most difficult for me to clear.

**Step 2**. I attempt that wave with the worst possible team that I still think has a chance to clear. ([For the hardest wave, I borrow a whale support](#claim-your-free-alert-5-clear).)

**Step 3a**. If I fail, I improve the team a bit (by adding stronger supports, wheels, etc.) and try again.

**Step 3b**. If I succeed, I move on to the next most difficult wave.

If there's a wave I can't beat, I aim to clear it twice at a lower difficulty, once again using the worst teams possible.

This strategy lets you maximize the resources you have for beating the hardest stages, while ensuring that you have enough left over to beat the easiest stages.

## Just Try It

I've given all the D-tide advice I can possibly give. But even with this advice, you may still be worried. You may ask: "What if D-tide is still too difficult?"

When I compare players who mainly do Alert 4 and players who mainly do Alert 5, the biggest difference is that Alert 5 players don't believe in the concept of "too difficult."

Yes, the enemy HP is crazy in Alert 5. Yes, the amount of incoming damage is stupid. Yes, you'll probably fail the first time you attempt it. But is that a reason not to try?

[Above](#choosing-your-difficulty), you can see screenshots of teams that cleared each D-tide alert with minimal investment. Those players didn't succeed because they spent more money. They didn't succeed because they were "more skilled," as if skill in Morimens is something you're born with. They succeeded because *they kept trying and proved it was possible*.

If you aren't sure whether you can clear a wave or not, **try it.**

In the worst-case scenario, you learn more about the challenges you're facing: what works, what doesn't, and what to watch out for next time.

In the best-case scenario, you clear the wave.
