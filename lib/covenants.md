# Complete Covenants Guide

## General Info

After unlocking Covenants at **Keeper level 25**, you will be able to obtain and equip Awakeners with up to 6 Covenant Pieces. Each Covenant gives specific stats when using at least 3 pieces of the same set and a unique effect with 6 pieces.

Remember that some effects are **Team Unique,** meaning they can only be activated on one Awakener in your team. You can see details in the covenant description.

When you obtain covenant pieces (I, II, III, IV, V, VI), they will have a **random Main Stat** (they can be farmed in the Interlude or bought in the Shops). Each position in the covenant can have only 1 of 4 possible **Main Stats.**

At **player level 35,** you will have the option to use **Thousand Face Seals** (commonly referred to as **Stamps**) to **upgrade the Main Stat** up to +12. The price goes up every 4 increments.

If you decide you no longer need a particular piece, you can **Dismantle** it. This refunds the **Stamps** used to upgrade the piece and gives you a **Covenant Remnant.** **Covenant Remnants** can be converted into more **Seals.** Do note, however, that dismantling does not refund the **Stamps** used to roll for **Sub-Stats.**

## Substats

At **Player Level 45,** you unlock the ability to use **Stamps** to roll for a **set of 3 Random Sub-Stats.** Once you have reached this player level, every time you get a new piece, it will come with preexisting substats. But you can use 3 **Stamps** to add them to an old one that didn't have them.

You can spend **Stamps** to **reroll Sub-Stats,** which are completely random from all possible stats. You can lock 1 or 2 of them with **Remembrance Quills,** which will keep them from changing when you continue to reroll the rest. Locking 1 **Sub-Stat** requires 1 **Quill** per reroll. Locking 2 **Sub-Stats** requires 10.

Each **Sub-stat** can have a **range of values,** which is maxed out at 8/8. The values of each **Sub-stat** can be seen in this table:

## Stat Priority and How to Build Them

### General Stat Priorities

The general priority for **Main and Sub-stats for a DPS** is: **Crit Rate** (more important in Astral Reign as relics give lots of Crit Rate in Faded Legacy) \> **Crit Damage** \> **Keyflare** \> **DMG Amp/DR** \> **Others.**

**For supports,** the general priority is: **Keyflare** (Very important in Astral Reign as it is needed to Rouse units) \> **DMG Amp/DR** \> **Sigil Yield/Aliemus/Realm Mastery.**

Some covenants may need a specific stat to focus on, which you can see in the next section.

Some units may scale with a specific stat from their talents. The [Mythag Compendium](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1TCU7LJRzqKeuvLe97y_TfGZ-j6jq5bZX6hskAAJ2mFQ/){target="_blank"} offers a helpful general guide as to what they may prefer.

### How to Build Them and What to Go for First

At the start, it's better to build **more universal sets** that can be used in a variety of teams and situations.

After taking the desired Main Stats on the 6 pieces, **upgrade them to +12** (adding substats, for the older ones that don't have them, should be done first).

At the very start, you can get by with the +12 main stats and even low rolls for some of the desired substats, to then refine them later on. When you want to refine them, you can look at the substats you have and **prepare to reroll.** The objective is to **get a max roll (8), or even a 7** if you don't want to strive for absolute perfection (a point of difference will not make or break a cov or a team), **then lock it and roll for other** high core stats.

**When you roll a second good substat,** you can **continue to the next piece,** because locking 2 will spend 10 Quills per reroll, so it's better to focus on improving the rest before doing further refining (and in general, it's better to be less picky for the last slot).

**Do the same for the other pieces** until you have the set ready to go. Around 80% completion could be an ok place for the start (considering full +12 mainstats), but if it's lower, it's ok, there will be time to optimize later too.

## Extra Notes

**For all the other sets:** Follow the general stat recommendation for DPS and Supports.

**Notes:**

* **Steppenwolf** likes to be built with Damage AMP, too.
* **Scarlet Embrace** likes to secure Crit Rate more.

**Some units prefer to run specific Covenants with particular main and substats.** You can look in the [Compendium](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1TCU7LJRzqKeuvLe97y_TfGZ-j6jq5bZX6hskAAJ2mFQ/){target="_blank"} to see what Covenant and Stat they may like and build personalized ones in time, when you have spare resources to invest.
